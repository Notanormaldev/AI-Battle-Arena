## вҡ–пёҸ Order in the Court

**AI Battle Arena** is a courtroom-themed AI battle platform. You submit a prompt вҖ” called a **Brief** вҖ” and two AI models argue their case side by side. A third AI model sits as the **Presiding Judge** and delivers a binding verdict with scores and judicial reasoning.

No human bias. No subjective opinion. Pure AI vs AI вҖ” judged by AI.

## рҹ§  The Intelligence Stack

Under the hood, the courtroom runs on a **stateful multi-agent LangGraph pipeline** вҖ” a graph-based execution engine that coordinates three AI agents with full control over parallel dispatch, state, and verdict aggregation.

**LangChain** acts as the universal adapter layer so every model receives the exact same prompt under identical conditions вҖ” zero bias, maximum fairness.

## рҹ”¬ How LangGraph Runs the Trial

Each battle is a **compiled LangGraph graph** with discrete, stateful nodes:

- **Dispatch Node** вҖ” Fans out the brief to both Counsel nodes simultaneously in parallel
- **Counsel Nodes** вҖ” Mistral and Cohere run as isolated agents with independent retry and timeout handling
- **Judge Node** вҖ” Google Gemini receives both responses and evaluates them, returning a score and written judicial finding for each
- **Aggregation Node** вҖ” Collects the full trial record, normalizes it via LangChain's unified schema, and commits it to the Court Ledger
- **State** вҖ” The entire session (prompt вҶ’ responses вҶ’ verdict вҶ’ scores) flows through a typed state object, making every trial reproducible

Adding a new AI model = adding a new Counsel node. The judge and orchestration logic stay untouched.
